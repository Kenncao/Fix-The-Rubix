We could work intuitively up until this point without memorizing any algorithms, but when it comes to solving the Middle layer (first two layers - F2L) of the Rubik's Cube, people frequently get stuck since there are too many moves to anticipate in order to complete this phase. You'll need to learn three algorithms to move the edge piece from the yellow layer to the second layer without causing any problems with the white face that has already been handled. We've been holding the cube with the white center facing up until now; now flip it over to review the puzzle more thoroughly.
